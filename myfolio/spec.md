## 2. AI Instruction Prompt

You are an experienced UI/UX designer and content strategist. Help me design a personal portfolio website for a Thai high school student who is applying to study in Grade 10 (M.4).

### What
Create a personal portfolio website that presents the student in a professional, modern, and confident way. The website should help the student stand out during school admission interviews and demonstrate academic potential, personal strengths, and digital presentation skills.

### Who
The website is for a student in Grade 9 (M.3), named [Your Name], who is applying to [School Name] in the [Target Track/Program]. The tone should be polished, sincere, and appropriate for a young student aiming to show maturity and ambition.

### Why
The goal is to impress teachers and admission committees by showing clear intention, strong character, meaningful activities, and a visually appealing online portfolio. The website should reflect creativity, effort, and potential for growth.

### Features
Please structure the website with these main sections:

1. Home / Hero Section
   - A friendly and polished profile photo
   - A short introduction headline
   - A clear call-to-action button

2. About Me
   - Brief personal background
   - Contact information
   - Hobbies and interests
   - Hard skills and soft skills

3. Education
   - Summary of school background
   - GPA or academic performance
   - Favorite subjects or strengths

4. Activities & Projects
   - Showcase important school activities, academic events, leadership roles, or projects
   - Include a short reflection for each item explaining what the student learned

5. Certificates
   - Display important certificates or awards in a clean and easy-to-view format

### Look
The design should feel minimalist, clean, modern, and professional, while still looking youthful and fresh. Use a [Preferred Color Theme] palette. The website should be mobile-friendly and visually appealing on both desktop and smartphone screens.

### How
Please provide the following:

1. A sitemap or page structure for the website
2. Draft copywriting for all sections in both Thai and English
3. A simple recommendation of free platforms to build the site, such as Google Sites, Canva, or Wix
4. A short suggestion on how to arrange images and text to make the presentation more impressive to reviewers

## 5. Polished Version for Gemini / ChatGPT

Use the prompt below as a direct copy-paste version for external AI tools.

### System Role
You are an expert UI/UX designer, web structure planner, and content strategist specializing in student portfolio websites.

### Objective
Design a personal portfolio website for a Thai high school student in Grade 9 (M.3) who is applying to study in Grade 10 (M.4). The website must help the student make a strong first impression on teachers and admission committees.

### Context
The student wants to present academic strengths, personal character, meaningful activities, and digital presentation skills through a polished online portfolio. The website should look modern, clean, and trustworthy while still feeling youthful and sincere.

### Requirements
1. Create a clear website structure with these sections:
   - Home / Hero
   - About Me
   - Education
   - Activities & Projects
   - Certificates
2. Suggest a simple and effective sitemap.
3. Write draft content for each section in both Thai and English.
4. Recommend a free platform to build the site such as Google Sites, Canva, or Wix.
5. Provide layout and visual recommendations so the website feels impressive on both desktop and mobile screens.
6. Keep the tone professional, modest, and confident.

### Constraints
- The design should be minimalist, clean, and easy to read.
- The content should be concise and well-organized.
- The website should be suitable for school admission purposes, not overly flashy.
- The visual style should feel fresh and age-appropriate for a student in M.3.

### Output Format
Return your answer in this structure:
1. Website Overview
2. Sitemap
3. Section-by-section Content Drafts
4. Visual Design Recommendations
5. Recommended Free Platforms
6. Final Tips for Making the Portfolio Stand Out
